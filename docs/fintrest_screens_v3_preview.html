Open question — letter grade vs. 0–100?

WallStreetZen collapses to A–F. Danelfin uses 1–10. We use 0–100. Letter is scannable; number is granular. Side-by-side below; our recommendation is dual display (letter for the glance, number for the drill-down).

Letter grade (WallStreetZen style)
A
Instant, scannable. "ALLY is an A." Doesn't survive the first tap — if A covers 85-100, all A's look the same.
0–100 (current Fintrest)
87/100
Granular. Lets a score of 87 feel different from 72 or 91. Harder to glance.
Proposed resolution: show both. Letter on list rows + heatmap (glance context). Number on ticker detail + signal cards (analysis context). Letter band = A (85+) · B (70–84) · C (55–69) · D (40–54) · F (<40).

Decision framework

① If paywall conversion matters more than category differentiation → blue. Finance is blue because users decide on "can I trust this?" in 2 seconds, and blue has 40 years of trust equity.
② If brand / editorial voice matters more (Lens is the product) → forest. Forest is what sets Fintrest apart visually; changing it means the "Moleskine of finance" framing dies.
③ Hybrid (my recommendation): keep forest as brand, adopt the 4-family content palette from the Palette tab. Visual gravity returns without burning differentiation. Re-test after shipping the palette expansion — if "too green" persists, revisit the rebrand.
