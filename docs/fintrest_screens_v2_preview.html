About Fintrest

We publish research. You decide what to do with it.

Fintrest is a stock research platform built on one unusual idea: most retail investing products try to tell you what to buy. We don't. We stress-test 500+ US stocks every morning, publish which ones passed and exactly why, and leave the decision where it belongs — with you.

Why we built this

The retail stock software market converged on one format: an app tells you what to buy, promises returns, and takes a subscription. The format works for a few weeks until the user loses three trades and churns. Then they try the next one. Retention in this category is brutal — often under 30% at six months.

We think the format is wrong. The problem isn't that people need better picks. It's that people need to understand the research, not just consume it. A signal you can't explain isn't research — it's a trade recommendation dressed up as one. We built Fintrest the other way: every score is explainable, every factor is audited, every outcome is logged publicly — losers included.

Our principles

Three things we will not do.

01

We don't recommend trades.

Fintrest publishes research. Every signal is a scored research artifact, not a recommendation. Lens explains what the model saw — never what you should do.

02

We don't hide losers.

Every signal's outcome is logged and published — hit rate, average move, drawdown, losers included. The audit log is public and it's complete.

03

We don't sell your data.

We don't share, rent, or sell user data. We make money from subscriptions, not from surveillance. If you cancel, your data is deleted.

Who we are

Fintrest is built by DSYS Inc., a technology company focused on AI, data engineering, and consumer fintech. We are not a Registered Investment Adviser. We do not manage money or execute trades. We build software, publish research, and leave the investing to you.

We're based in Alpharetta, Georgia, and we're a small team. You can reach us anytime at deep@dsysinc.com.
