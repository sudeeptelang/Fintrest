01Positioning & guardrails

Fintrest.ai publishes research, not recommendations. This is not a tagline — it is the legal posture that keeps the product inside the publisher's exemption and out of Investment Adviser Act territory. Every UX decision in this document is downstream of that posture.

Hard UX rules

These must be true on every screen, in every Lens narration, in every push, email, SMS, and social share:

  • The literal word BUY never appears as a directive next to a ticker. Badges read In research set, Passed, High score, or Setup active.
  • The words sell, buy this, recommend, you should, go long, go short never appear in Lens output. Lens describes; it does not direct.
  • Every signal displays source + staleness where lag matters — SEC EDGAR · 2d ago, FINRA · 9d old, Quiver · up to 45d STOCK Act lag. Hiding lag destroys trust.
  • Backtest numbers and live numbers are never blended in the same stat.
  • Every page with analyst targets, historical signal outcomes, or backtest stats carries the standard disclaimer footer.
  • The audit log is public, includes losers, and is always one tap/click away. Never hidden, collapsed, or paywalled.
  • Fake testimonials are forbidden. Name real beta users with linkable profiles, or publish no testimonials.
Regulatory-sensitive surfaces — legal review required Three product areas sit closest to the adviser/publisher boundary: Lens Personalized (Elite tier), Ask Lens chat, and alert templates. Get a securities attorney to sign off on the publisher's-exemption posture for each before launch. The Lens Personalized language ("thesis tuned to your holdings and risk profile") is the most adviser-adjacent copy in the product — consider reframing as "portfolio lens" (Lens applied to holdings) without the word "tuned."

03Design system

Color tokens

All UI colors come from named tokens with light + dark values. Never use raw hex inline.

TokenLightDarkUsage
--fin-accent#0F6E56#9FE1CBBrand, positive outcomes, primary action
--fin-accent-bgrgba(15,110,86,0.07)rgba(159,225,203,0.08)Accent card backgrounds
--fin-accent-borderrgba(15,110,86,0.25)rgba(159,225,203,0.25)Accent card borders
--fin-warn#BA7517#EF9F27Caution, middling scores (50–70), flags
--fin-danger#A32D2D#F09595Negative outcomes, losses, stopped-out signals
--fin-info#185FA5#85B7EBNeutral info states
Color semantics, not decoration Green = accent/positive, amber = warning/middling, red = negative. Never use more colors. Scores 0–49 are red, 50–69 amber, 70+ green. This is a product convention that makes the whole UI legible at a glance.

Typography

  • Lens thesis + editorial copy uses serif (Georgia / Iowan / Palatino fallback). This is the one place serif appears — it signals "research analyst voice," not UI chrome.
  • Everything else uses system sans (SF Pro / Segoe UI).
  • Numbers in tables and stat cards use tabular numerals: font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums.
  • Two weights only: 400 regular, 500–600 medium. Never 700 bold.

Spacing & corners

  • Border radius: 6px for inputs/small elements, 8px for cards, 12px for containers, 20px for phone mockups, 999px for pills.
  • Borders: 0.5px for tertiary (default), 1px for primary containers, 2px accent only for "selected" or "featured" emphasis.
  • Shadows: none. Elevation is communicated through border + background contrast, not shadow.

Score ring — the signature component

The 8-segment score ring is Fintrest's visual signature. Segment length = segment fraction of circumference; fill length within the segment = score 0–100. Every segment has a neutral track (15% opacity accent) and a filled arc (solid accent). Segments are: Momentum, Rel volume, News catalyst, Earnings, Sentiment, Trend, Risk, Smart money — always in that order, always with Smart money last.

04Content voice — Lens

Lens is Fintrest's research narrator. Every factor score has a Lens paragraph. Every ticker has a thesis. Every signal has reasoning. The voice must be consistent across hundreds of signals written by an LLM without sounding like an LLM.

The rules

  • Specific over generic. "The CFO's $2.4M purchase — her largest since joining in 2023" beats "insiders are buying."
  • Named over anonymous. "Tiger Global initiated a $180M position" beats "a major hedge fund."
  • Numbers with context. "Call volume 4.1x the 30-day average" beats "unusual options activity."
  • Describe, don't direct. "The setup reflects..." not "you should buy because..."
  • Admit uncertainty. "The 13F reports with a 45-day lag, so this position may have changed."
  • One sentence of context per data point maximum. No editorializing.

Tone anchor

MSFT breaks out on 94th-percentile news catalyst activity alongside real insider accumulation — CFO and two directors bought a combined $4.2M in open-market shares in the last 30 days, the CFO's largest purchase since joining in 2023. Tiger Global initiated a $180M position in the latest 13F. Unusual call buying on the Nov 22 expiry (4.1x average, ask-weighted) confirms institutional positioning ahead of the setup.

That is the voice. Every Lens output should sound like it was written by that same analyst. Read it back — if it sounds like a GPT wrapper, rewrite it.

09Signal deep-dive: per-factor explainers

The 8-factor breakdown shows scores. The deep-dive shows why. Tapping any factor row from the ticker page opens a dedicated explainer that breaks down what the score is measuring, what the inputs looked like, how this compares to the universe, and what Lens reads into it. This is the page that separates Fintrest from every competitor's black-box score.

Deep-dive architecture Each of the 8 factor deep-dives follows the same 5-block structure: (1) Factor summary — score, what it measures, how it's computed. (2) Inputs panel — the specific data points with numbers. (3) Distribution — where this ticker sits in the universe. (4) Historical trajectory — how this factor score has evolved over 90 days. (5) Lens commentary — the narrative interpretation.

What lives on every factor deep-dive page

Each of the 8 deep-dive pages is structurally identical — only the inputs and interpretation change:

  1. Factor summary card — score + circular progress + one-sentence description + universe percentile. Top of every deep-dive.
  2. Inputs panel — 6–8 stat cards showing the specific measurements that feed the score, each with the universe median or comparable context. These are the numbers. Not decoration.
  3. Distribution band — horizontal band showing weak/average/strong terciles with the current ticker's position marked. Instant "where do I stand" context.
  4. 90-day trajectory — line chart of this factor's score over 90 days with universe median reference line. Shows whether the current reading is emerging, stable, or decaying.
  5. Lens commentary — 2–3 sentence narrative that names the dominant driver. Every deep-dive ends with this.

Unique elements for specific factors

FactorAdditional element
News catalystList of top 5 catalyst articles with source badges (Benzinga, Reuters, EDGAR 8-K)
Earnings quality4-quarter beat/miss grid + next earnings countdown + implied move
TrendMini price chart showing MSFT price with 50d and 200d MAs overlaid
RiskIV rank gauge, beta vs. SPY, 90-day drawdown line

19Methodology & help

The methodology page is load-bearing. It's linked from every signal, the public landing page, and the audit log. It exists to pass the "is this legitimate?" test from a skeptical reader in 90 seconds.

Page outline

  1. How a signal is made — 3-paragraph walkthrough of the 8-factor scoring pipeline. No equations; plain English.
  2. The eight factors — one-paragraph explainer per factor with canonical data sources.
  3. The universal bar — what composite score + factor-floor thresholds a ticker must pass to enter today's drop. Made explicit, not hand-waved.
  4. Data sources & freshness — table of every source (Polygon, EDGAR, FRED, Benzinga, Quiver, UW, FINRA, Stocktwits) with update frequency, lag, and which factor it feeds.
  5. Regime model — how risk-on/off is classified and which weights shift under each regime.
  6. Audit log philosophy — why losers are published, failure-mode tagging, what "closed" means.
  7. What Fintrest is not — the publisher's exemption language; not an adviser, not a broker, not discretionary.
  8. Backtest caveats — survivorship bias discussion, regime coverage, out-of-sample split.
Methodology builds conversion This is the highest-trust page in the product. Every beta user who signed up cited reading methodology before clicking. Budget design effort accordingly — this is not a throwaway help doc.

Route conventions

  • Tickers are always uppercase in URLs: /ticker/MSFT, not /ticker/msft. 301 redirect lowercase to canonical.
  • Signal IDs are stable integers. Never reuse an ID even after a signal closes.
  • Board slugs are user-generated, kebab-case: /my/boards/ai-infrastructure.
  • Factor names in URLs are canonical: momentum, rel-volume, news, earnings, sentiment, trend, risk, smart-money.
